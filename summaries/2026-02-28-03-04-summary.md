Recent AI coverage centers on the accelerating tensions between AI capability and accountability, with coverage spanning corporate policy disputes, agent security vulnerabilities, and the societal consequences of rapid deployment. Industry governance emerges as a recurring thread, from conflicts over military AI safeguards and corporate misconduct to debates about whether leading labs truly understand the systems they are building. A significant cluster of articles examines AI agents specifically — their growing autonomy, the novel attack surfaces they introduce, and the design principles needed to keep humans in the loop. Parallel to these concerns, contributors reflect on AI's transformative pressure on creative and knowledge work, questioning what roles remain distinctly human as models begin to self-improve and agentic platforms displace traditional workflows. The collection closes on a note of deliberate ambiguity, with dueling forecasts for a near future marked either by an intelligence crisis or an intelligence boom, underscoring the unresolved uncertainty that defines this moment in AI development.
